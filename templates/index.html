Welcome to Water Prediction System

Ensure water safety with our AI-powered prediction system. By analyzing key water quality parameters,
we provide insights to help you make informed decisions about water consumption and management.

Features Detail

pH

Description

pH measures the acidity or alkalinity of water on a scale from 0 to 14. A pH of 7 is neutral, values below 7 indicate acidity, and values above 7 indicate alkalinity.

Iron (Fe)

Description

Iron is a metal commonly found in water due to natural sources, corroded pipes, or industrial waste. It can cause a metallic taste, staining, and discoloration in plumbing and laundry.

Nitrate (NO₃⁻)

Description

Nitrate is a compound containing nitrogen and oxygen, often found in fertilizers, sewage, and industrial waste. High levels in drinking water can cause health risks, especially for infants.

Chloride (Cl⁻)

Description

Chloride is a naturally occurring ion in water, often from salt deposits, seawater intrusion, or industrial pollution. High concentrations can lead to a salty taste and corrosion of metal pipes.

Turbidity

Description

Turbidity is the measure of suspended particles such as silt, clay, and microorganisms in water. Higher turbidity reduces water clarity, affects taste, and may indicate contamination.

Fluoride (F⁻)

Description

Fluoride is a mineral naturally present in water and sometimes added to prevent tooth decay. While beneficial in small amounts, excessive levels can cause dental and skeletal fluorosis.

Copper (Cu)

Description

Copper is a metal that can enter water through corrosion of copper pipes, natural deposits, or industrial waste. High levels can cause health issues such as nausea and staining.

Odor

Description

Odor in water can arise from organic matter, bacterial growth, or chemical contamination. Strong or unusual smells may indicate pollution, requiring further investigation.

Chlorine (Cl₂)

Description

Chlorine is a disinfectant commonly used in water treatment to eliminate bacteria and viruses. While essential for safety, excessive chlorine can cause a strong taste and irritation.

Manganese (Mn)

Description

Manganese is a mineral naturally occurring in groundwater, often found alongside iron. High levels can cause black staining, unpleasant taste, and potential health concerns.

Total Dissolved Solids

Description

TDS measures the total concentration of dissolved minerals, salts, and metals in water. Elevated levels can affect taste, cause scaling in pipes, and indicate potential contamination.
